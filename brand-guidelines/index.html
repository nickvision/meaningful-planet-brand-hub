Brand Overview

Who we are, what we stand for, and the principles that guide every design and copy decision.

Our Vision

To build a world that works better.

Smarter telecoms for a world that works better. Building a world that works better through radical clarity and proactive action.

Elevator Pitch

SMEs globally waste billions in telecoms spend due to black-box billing, inefficient platforms, and excessive charges. Meaningful Planet solves this with an intelligent telecoms management platform with bundled connectivity, using machine learning to deliver clarity, control, and proactive savings – with sustainability fully integrated into the model.

What We Do

Meaningful Planet is an all-in-one mobile connectivity solution for businesses – combining mobile network, intelligent telecoms management platform, and sustainability into a single service. We give organisations complete visibility and control over their mobile estate. Costs come down, admin is simplified, and contracts finally work in the customer's favour.

Every service also funds meaningful nature restoration projects across the UK, delivered with our partners, the regional Wildlife Trusts. With emissions tracking and offsetting built in as standard, we're a carbon-neutral network that delivers better for customers and the planet.

Who We Serve

Our sweet spot is SMEs managing mobile estates of up to approximately 1,500 SIMs. These are businesses large enough to have real telecoms complexity – multiple lines, varied data plans, ongoing cost management – but without dedicated telecoms departments or enterprise procurement processes.

Primary users of the Milo platform include operations managers, IT leads, office managers, and finance teams who are responsible for telecoms alongside other duties. They need tools that are intuitive and efficient, not enterprise-grade complexity that requires specialist training. Secondary users include the business mobile end users themselves, who interact with their individual SIM details and account settings.

Positioning note: We are not an enterprise provider. Our language, pricing structures, and product decisions should reflect SME needs and SME buying behaviour. Avoid enterprise-oriented framing like "enterprise-grade" in customer-facing materials. Internally, we may reference that we deliver enterprise-quality service at SME scale – but externally, lead with simplicity, clarity, and value.

Product Vision

An intelligent telecoms solution with integrated connectivity that improves efficiency and provides customers with control, clarity, and confidence.

Logo

Our logo system includes the Meaningful Planet brand mark and the Milo product wordmark.

Meaningful Planet – Brand Mark

The Meaningful Planet mark is an organic, flowing form that evokes growth, connectivity, and the natural world. It uses two tones from our palette to create depth and movement. The mark should always be presented as a complete unit – never crop, rotate, or separate its elements.

Light backgrounds
Dark backgrounds
Mono – Black
Mono – White

Clear Space and Minimum Size

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of one "leaf" element within the mark. Minimum reproduction size: 32px height on screen, 12mm in print.

Logo Misuse

Do Not
Rotate, skew, or distort the mark

Apply drop shadows, glows, or 3D effects

Place on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid container

Change the colours to anything outside the approved variants

Separate or rearrange the mark's elements

Add outlines or strokes to the mark
Always
Use the correct variant for the background

Maintain the clear space zone

Use the provided SVG or PNG files – never recreate the mark

Ensure sufficient contrast between the mark and its background

Milo – Product Wordmark

Milo is the name of the SIM management platform. Its wordmark features the leaf-circle icon alongside the "MILO" logotype. Use within the product (sidebar, favicon) and on product-specific materials. It should not replace the Meaningful Planet brand mark on corporate or marketing materials.

When to use which: Meaningful Planet mark for the website, emails, partner materials, and all external-facing brand contexts. Milo wordmark within the platform itself and on product-specific documentation.

AI-generated copy: When using AI tools to draft copy, always specify British English and review output carefully. US spellings are the most common error in AI-generated content. Check every '-ize', '-or', and '-er' ending.

Key Punctuation Rules

Do
Use the Oxford comma: "SIMs, subscriptions, and invoices"

Use contractions in UI and web copy: "You can't suspend a terminated SIM"

Use curly (smart) quotes and apostrophes

Use en dash (–) for ranges: "100–1,500 SIMs"

Use en dash with spaces for parenthetical asides where commas would be ambiguous: "Our platform – built for SMEs – gives you full control"

End all complete sentences with full stops in descriptions, tooltips, and body text

Periods in website hero headings are acceptable as a stylistic choice (e.g., "Full visibility. Real impact.")
Do Not
Use full stops on buttons, nav items, badges, or column headers

Use ALL CAPS anywhere – for emphasis, use font weight

Use em dashes (—) anywhere – we exclusively use en dashes (–) or commas

Use ampersands in body text or button labels

Use purely numeric date formats (01/01/2026) – use "1 January 2026"

Use US English spellings – British English only

Use exclamation marks in UI copy (rare exceptions in marketing, very sparingly)

Website Navigation

The main navigation uses the Moss background with white Figtree text. Primary sections visible in the nav: Home, Business, Personal, Coverage, Our Impact, About, Login. The Meaningful Planet brand mark sits at the left. Navigation items are title case, Figtree medium weight.

Website Buttons

Website CTAs follow a dual-button pattern: a primary action (Sapling or Moss filled) alongside a secondary action (outline/ghost). This gives users a clear primary path while offering alternatives.

On light backgrounds
Get a Tailored Quote → Book a Demo See MILO in Action →
On dark backgrounds
Get a Tailored Quote → See MILO in Action
Arrow convention: The primary CTA uses a trailing arrow (→) to suggest forward momentum. Secondary/exploratory CTAs do not include the arrow. The arrow character is a Unicode right arrow (→), not a greater-than sign (>).

Trust Signals

The website includes a social proof logo bar below the hero showing customer/partner logos. These are displayed in a muted, single-tone treatment (grayscale or Pine-tinted) so they don't compete with the brand palette. The bar may include a label like "Trusted by businesses across the UK".

Photography Direction

Website photography falls into two categories: nature/restoration imagery (used in hero sections and impact banners – forests, peatlands, wildlife, real project sites) and workplace imagery (used in feature sections – real teams, real offices, natural and unstaged). Avoid generic stock photography of handshakes, posed teams, or manicured nature scenes. All photography should use a natural, slightly warm colour grade that complements the Chalk and Moss palette.

Applications

How the brand system is applied across emails, the Milo platform, partner materials, and more.

Email Templates

Transactional and notification emails follow a consistent structure:

Email anatomy
Header: Moss background, gradient accent, logo + category label, H1 in white
Action card: Gradient top border, category label, CTA button
Stat cards: Mist background, 3-column, values in Moss bold
CTA
Final CTA: Moss card, Sapling button, reinforces primary action
Email button styles: Sapling (#D2FF85) with Moss text on dark sections. Moss with white text in white sections. Always border-radius: 8px, font-weight: 600–700, and include the arrow: "Activate Account →"

Milo Platform

The Milo platform currently uses a Tailwind-based design system (emerald scale, gray-50 backgrounds) that predates the formalised brand palette. The copy rules, Figtree typography, and voice/tone guidelines apply to Milo immediately. Colour palette migration is a separate workstream.

Migration note: Do not force brand colours into the existing Tailwind system without a coordinated migration plan. Copy, typography, and voice changes can and should be applied independently and immediately.

Partner and Co-Brand Materials

When creating materials alongside partner brands, use the Meaningful Planet mark on white or Chalk backgrounds. Keep our palette neutral – Pine for accents rather than the full palette – to avoid visual competition. Partner logos should be given equal or greater visual weight. The Wildlife Trusts should be referenced by name in any sustainability-related materials.

Social Media

Social media graphics use the Moss/Sapling combination for maximum recognition. Profile images use the Meaningful Planet mark (colour on light variant). Use Figtree for text overlays. Maintain the brand voice – professional but human, never corporate-speak or hashtag-stuffed.
